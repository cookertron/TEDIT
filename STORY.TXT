The Last Bug

Ray stared at the terminal, his coffee long cold.

Three hours. Three hours on a Hello World program. The 6800 should have
made it simple. Load the address, output the character, loop. But the
screen just showed garbage, random characters dancing where his
greeting should be.

He had checked the ACIA configuration twice. Baud rate, word length,
stop bits, all correct. He had verified his string was stored at the
right address. He had traced through the loop with a pencil and paper,
counting cycles, following the program counter by hand.

Nothing.

"It has to be the status register," he muttered, pulling the
Motorola reference manual closer. Page 47. ACIA status register,
bit 1: Transmit Data Register Empty. His eyes drifted to his code.

There it was. He was testing bit 0 instead of bit 1. A single bit.
His BIT instruction was using the wrong mask, 01 instead of 02.

He reached for the keyboard, changed one byte, and hit RUN.

  Hello World!

The cursor blinked patiently after the exclamation mark. Ray leaned
back in his chair and laughed. Eight bits wide, and he had picked
the wrong one.

He poured his cold coffee down the sink and made a fresh cup. Tomorrow
he would start on the serial driver. But tonight, those two words on
the screen were enough.
